# Connect a cluster to Calico Cloud Free

CONCEPT: Connecting your cluster to the Calico Cloud free tier (What’s happening under the hood, what changes in your cluster, what happens to data, what do you get, etc.)

## System requirements

* You have an active Calico Cloud Free account.
  To sign up, go to [HERE](#).
* You are signed in to the web console.
* You have kubectl access to a Kubernetes cluster.
* You installed Calico Open Source 3.30 or later on your cluster. (CNI? Network policy only?)
* Make sure you allow TCP to \<domain>.calicocloud.io:443.

Nil

PROCEDURE: Connect your cluster

1. From the web console, click **Connect a cluster**.
1. On the **Name your cluster** dialog, enter a name and click **Continue**.
1. On the **You're ready to connect!** dialog, copy the kubectl command.
   <details>
   <summary>Example kubectl command</summary>
   ```bash
   kubectl apply -f - <<EOF
   # Once applied to your managed cluster, a deployment is created to establish a secure tcp connection
   # with the management cluster.

   apiVersion: operator.tigera.io/v1
   kind: ManagementClusterConnection
   metadata:
   name: tigera-secure
   spec:
   # ManagementClusterAddr should be the externally reachable address to which your managed cluster
   # will connect. Valid examples are: "0.0.0.0:31000", "example.com:32000", "[::1]:32500"
   managementClusterAddr: "oss-ui-01-management.dev.calicocloud.io:443"

   ---

   apiVersion: v1
   kind: Secret
   metadata:
   name: tigera-managed-cluster-connection
   namespace: tigera-operator
   type: Opaque
   data:
   # This is the certificate of the management cluster side of the tunnel.
   management-cluster.crt: ...
   # The certificate and private key that are created and signed by the CA in the management cluster.
   managed-cluster.crt: ...
   managed-cluster.key: ...

   ---
   apiVersion: v1
   kind: Secret
   metadata:
   name: tigera-voltron-linseed-certs-public
   namespace: tigera-operator
   type: Opaque
   data:
   tls.crt: ...

   EOF
   ```
   </details>

1. From the terminal, paste and run the kubectl command.
